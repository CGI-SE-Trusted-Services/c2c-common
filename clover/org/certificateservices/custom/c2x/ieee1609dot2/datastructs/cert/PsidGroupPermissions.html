20    /**
21    * This structure states the permissions that a certificate holder has with respect to issuing and requesting
22    * certficates for a particular set of PSIDs. In this structure:
23    * <p>
24    * <li>subjectPermissions indicates PSIDs and SSP ranges covered by this field.
25    * <li>minChainDepth and chainDepthRange indicate how long the certificate chain from this certificate to the
26    * end-entity certificate is permitted to be. The length of the certificate chain is measured from the certificate
27    * issued by this certificate to the end-entity certificate in the case of certIssuePermissions and from the certificate
28    * requested by this certificate to the end-entity certificate in the case of certRequestPermissions;
29    * a length of 0 therefore indicates that the certificate issued or requested is an end-entity certificate. The length
30    * is permitted to be (a) greater than or equal to minChainDepth certificates and (b) less than or equal to
31    * minChainDepth + chainDepthRange certificates. The value -1 for chainDepthRange is a special case: if the
32    * value of chainDepthRange is -1 that indicates that the certificate chain may be any length equal to or
33    * greater than minChainDepth.
34    * <li>eeType takes one or more of the values app and enrol and indicates the type of certificates or requests that this
35    * instance of PsidGroupPermissions in the certificate is entitled to authorize. If this field indicates app, the chain ends
36    * in an authorization certificate, i.e. a certficate in which these permissions appear in an appPermissions field.
37    * If this field indicates enrol, the chain ends in an enrolment certificate, i.e. a certificate in which these permissions
38    * appear in a certReqPermissions permissions field), or both. Different instances of PsidGroupPermissions within a
39    * ToBeSignedCertificate may have different values for eeType.
40    *
41    * @author Philip Vendil, p.vendil@cgi.com
42    *
43    */
