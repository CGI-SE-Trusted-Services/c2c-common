18    * The elevation field represents the geographic position above or below the WGS84 ellipsoid. The 16-bit value is
19    * interpreted as an integer number of decimeters and represents an asymmetric range of positive and negative values.
20    * The encoding is as follows: the range 0x0000 to 0xEFFF (0 to 61439 decimal) are positive numbers representing
21    * elevations from 0 to +6143.9 m (i.e., above the reference ellipsoid). The range 0xF001 to 0xFFFF are negative
22    * numbers representing elevations from 409.5 m to 0.1 m (i.e., below the reference ellipsoid). An elevation
23    * higher than +6143.9 m is represented 0xEFFF. An elevation lower than 409.5 m is represented 0xF001.
24    * An elevation data element of 0xF000 corresponds to unknown elevation.
25    *
26    * Examples of this encoding are: the elevation 0 m is encoded as 0x0000.
27    * The elevation 0.1 m is encoded as 0xFFFF.
